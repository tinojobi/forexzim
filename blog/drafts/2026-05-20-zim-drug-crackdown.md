<p>Zimbabwe’s nationwide crackdown on drug and substance abuse has delivered significant law‑enforcement and social‑intervention results, with authorities reporting 2,889 arrests, 381 convictions, and the seizure of drugs worth nearly ZiG80 million.</p>

<p>The figures were disclosed during the 15th post‑Cabinet media briefing in Harare on 19 May 2026, where Defence Minister Oppah Muchinguri‑Kashiri presented the report to Cabinet. The operation, conducted under the Multi‑Sectoral Drug and Substance Abuse Plan 2024‑2030, targeted suppliers, end‑users, and illegal drug hubs in Manicaland and Mashonaland West provinces. According to Cabinet, approximately 453 drug suppliers, 2,436 individual end‑users, and 31 drug bases were dismantled in those districts (<a href="https://iharare.com/zimbabwe-in-shock-as-2-889-arrested-in-massive-zig80-million-drug-bust/">iHarare</a>).</p>

<p>The seized substances included crystal meth, cocaine, ecstasy tablets, dagga, khat, and unregistered medicines such as cough syrups, with a total value of ZiG 79 931 130 (<a href="https://iharare.com/zimbabwe-in-shock-as-2-889-arrested-in-massive-zig80-million-drug-bust/">iHarare</a>). Law‑enforcement agencies also carried out a sweep of liquor‑licensed premises, inspecting 615 outlets nationwide; 36 unlicensed venues were fined and closed for operating without proper authorization (<a href="https://iharare.com/zimbabwe-in-shock-as-2-889-arrested-in-massive-zig80-million-drug-bust/">iHarare</a>).</p>

<p>Beyond arrests and seizures, the government emphasized prevention, rehabilitation, and socioeconomic interventions. Awareness and prevention campaigns reached 285,918 beneficiaries across communities and schools (<a href="https://iharare.com/zimbabwe-in-shock-as-2-889-arrested-in-massive-zig80-million-drug-bust/">iHarare</a>). Drug‑testing kits were acquired to broaden screening capabilities, while social workers facilitated the re‑integration of 171 children with their families and removed 32 street‑living children from harmful environments (<a href="https://iharare.com/zimbabwe-in-shock-as-2-889-arrested-in-massive-zig80-million-drug-bust/">iHarare</a>). Approximately 37,937 youths and women have already benefited from rehabilitation, entrepreneurship, and training programmes aimed at providing economic alternatives to drug involvement (<a href="https://iharare.com/zimbabwe-in-shock-as-2-889-arrested-in-massive-zig80-million-drug-bust/">iHarare</a>).</p>

<p>As part of the broader strategy, a US$1 million youth empowerment fund (approximately R18.2 million) was launched to support skills training, job creation, and small‑business grants for at‑risk youths (<a href="https://iharare.com/zimbabwe-in-shock-as-2-889-arrested-in-massive-zig80-million-drug-bust/">iHarare</a>). The fund complements existing rehabilitation and treatment centres, which have been expanded to increase access to counselling and psychosocial support.</p>

<p>Officials noted that a significant number of cases are progressing through the courts, with 2,113 accused persons already arraigned and further convictions expected as enforcement efforts tighten (<a href="https://www.zimbabwenow.co.zw/articles/21948/drug-crackdown-nets-3193-suspects/">Zimbabwe Now</a>). The crackdown reflects a whole‑of‑government approach, combining law‑enforcement disruption of supply chains with demand‑reduction through socio‑economic initiatives, all guided by the Multi‑Sectoral Drug and Substance Abuse Plan 2024‑2030 (<a href="https://cite.org.zw/zim-drug-crackdown-over-3-000-arrested-52-drug-bases-shut-down/">CITEZW</a>).</p>
