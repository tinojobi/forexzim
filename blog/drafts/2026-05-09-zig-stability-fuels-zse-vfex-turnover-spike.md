<p>The Zimbabwe Stock Exchange (ZSE) has attributed a massive spike in trading activity directly to the macroeconomic stability brought about by the ZiG currency. During the first quarter of 2026, dropping inflation and a predictable exchange rate ignited a surge of investor confidence across both the local and foreign currency stock markets.</p>

<h2>ZiG Anchors the Recovery</h2>

<p>According to ZSE Company Secretary Lyndon Nkomo, presenting the <a href="https://www.newzimbabwe.com/zse-credits-zig-stability-for-increased-trades-on-stock-markets/">quarterly trading update for the period ended March 31, 2026</a>, Zimbabwe's macroeconomic landscape has shown sustained resilience. Month-on-month ZiG inflation dropped from 4.1% in January to 3.8% in February, marking the first time the country has maintained single-digit inflation in decades.</p>

<p>Furthermore, the official <a href="https://zimrate.com/history">exchange rate history</a> shows the ZiG holding stable at around 25.57 to the US dollar throughout the quarter. This stability has replaced the previous volatility with market predictability, drawing investors back into the capital markets.</p>

<h2>Record-Breaking Quarter</h2>

<p>The stability translated directly into numbers. The ZSE saw a transformative surge in secondary market activity, with aggregate turnover for Q1 2026 hitting US$242.81 million. This represents an extraordinary 416.83% increase compared to the final quarter of 2025.</p>

<p>The US-dollar-denominated Victoria Falls Stock Exchange (VFEX) also achieved historic milestones:</p>

<ul>
    <li>Total market capitalization hit a record high of US$4 billion.</li>
    <li>Total value traded rose by 69.35% to reach US$36.38 million.</li>
    <li>The VFEX All Share Index registered a year-to-date appreciation of 41.07%.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Overcoming Headwinds</h2>

<p>The stellar Q1 performance wasn't without its challenges. The Group navigated a significant shift following the delisting of Econet Wireless Zimbabwe from the main ZSE board at the end of the quarter. However, the impact was partially offset by new listings on the VFEX, helping to maintain the overall number of listed entities at 66 across both bourses.</p>
