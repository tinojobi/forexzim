# Research Brief

## STORY
A generic global explainer on dollarisation can be turned into a Zimbabwe-specific banking and finance story by tying it to local trust, deposits, lending, and SME funding conditions.

## PRIMARY KEYWORD
why Zimbabwe businesses flock to the dollar

## CLUSTER ID
2026-06-06-zimbabwe-dollarisation-trust-credit

## KEY FACTS
- [HIGH] The Zimbabwe Mail piece is republishing an Al Majalla explainer whose core thesis is that people move into US dollars when local currencies stop functioning as a store of value, unit of account, and trusted medium of exchange.
- [HIGH] Reuters reported in July 2024 that foreign currencies were still used in about 80% of local transactions in Zimbabwe, while ZiG accounted for about 20%.
- [HIGH] Reuters reported ZiG was Zimbabwe's sixth attempt at a local currency in 15 years, underlining the trust deficit created by repeated currency failures.
- [HIGH] The RBZ 2025 Monetary Policy Statement says foreign currency deposits constituted 83% of total deposits in December 2024.
- [HIGH] The RBZ 2025 Monetary Policy Statement says foreign currency loans accounted for 88% of total private-sector credit.
- [HIGH] The RBZ 2025 Monetary Policy Statement says the liquidity challenges partly reflected the inclination of economic agents in a dollarised economy to spend local currency while reserving foreign currency for store-of-value purposes.
- [HIGH] The RBZ 2025 Monetary Policy Statement says the Targeted Finance Facility was introduced to support productive sectors and improve the flow of funds in the interbank market.
- [HIGH] The RBZ 2025 Monetary Policy Statement says productive sectors received 72.25% of total loans as at 31 December 2024.

## KEY QUOTES
- Reuters: the current transaction split was about 20% ZiG and 80% foreign currencies in mid-2024.
- RBZ: liquidity challenges partly reflected the tendency in a dollarised economy to spend local currency while reserving foreign currency for store-of-value purposes.
- Al Majalla: currency collapse is fundamentally a crisis of trust, not only a technical monetary event.

## CONTEXT NEEDED FOR READER
- Dollarisation is not only about household savings. It changes how banks gather deposits, price loans, manage liquidity, and extend working-capital credit.
- SMEs care because suppliers, rent, imports, and inventory often require hard currency or pricing linked to it.
- If deposits and loans are overwhelmingly in foreign currency, local-currency credit stays harder to deepen even when policymakers want more ZiG usage.
- The Zimbabwe angle is stronger than the raw republished article because there is current official and independent evidence showing dollar dominance in transactions, deposits, and lending.
