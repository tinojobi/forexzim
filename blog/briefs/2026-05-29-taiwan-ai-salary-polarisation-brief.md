STORY: Skills Become the New Currency: Salary Polarisation Deepens as AI and Semiconductor Talent Command Up to 30% Pay Increases in Taiwan
CLUSTER_ID: global-labour-market-ai-skills

KEY FACTS (with confidence tags):
- [HIGH] Robert Walters Taiwan's 15th anniversary report finds AI/semiconductor specialists commanding salary increases of up to 30% when changing jobs, while execution-focused roles see moderate growth (Source: Robert Walters via Media OutReach Newswire + VIR Vietnam + Sina HK)
- [HIGH] Semiconductor/high-tech professionals changing jobs see 15-20% salary increases; AI, HPC, Edge Computing, and cross-border supply chain specialists can command up to 30% (Source: Robert Walters report via multiple outlets)
- [HIGH] 70% of Taiwanese employees earned below the average salary level in 2025, the highest proportion on record (Source: DGBAS via Taipei Times + Al Jazeera + Robert Walters report)
- [HIGH] Taiwan GDP grew 8.63% in 2025 and 13.69% in Q1 2026, driven by semiconductor/AI demand (Source: Al Jazeera citing official data)
- [HIGH] TSMC raising employee performance bonuses by 30%+ in 2026 to prevent talent leakage, after Samsung Electronics union secured large bonus agreement (Source: WSJ via MK Korea + multiple outlets)
- [HIGH] Taiwan's Central Bank Governor Yang Chin-lung warned of a "K-shaped economy" where tech sectors soar while others stagnate (Source: Al Jazeera)
- [HIGH] Semiconductors account for over 20% of Taiwan's GDP. TSMC alone represents over 40% of stock market value. Taiwan produces ~90% of world's most advanced chips (Source: Al Jazeera + US trade data)
- [HIGH] 40% of Taiwanese households feel financially "anxious" or "very anxious" due to rising living costs, particularly housing (Source: survey of 1,195 voters via Al Jazeera/Academia Sinica)
- [HIGH] Regular monthly wages averaged NT$48,768, up 2.6% YoY. Total compensation including bonuses/overtime rose 4.4% to NT$57,509, fastest growth in 16 years (Source: DGBAS via Taipei Times)
- [MEDIUM] Taiwan's Gini coefficient rose from 0.308 in 1980 (comparable to Norway) to 0.341 in 2024 (Source: Al Jazeera)
- [MEDIUM] Taiwan's talent market has shifted from employer-driven to candidate-driven. 54% of professionals cite learning/development as key reason to stay; 36% prioritize flexible working (Source: Robert Walters report)
