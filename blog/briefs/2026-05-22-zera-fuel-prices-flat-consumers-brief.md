# Research Brief: ZERA fuel prices stay flat as consumers watch oil shocks

## KEY FACTS

- [MEDIUM] iHarare reported that ZERA confirmed fuel prices remained unchanged amid social media rumours of a price change. The readable source content is limited, so use only as attributed support for the unchanged-prices angle.
- [HIGH] The Zimbabwean reported that ZERA said Diesel 50 would remain at US$2.09 per litre and Blend E20 at US$2.08 per litre for the 5 May to 15 May 2026 pricing window.
- [HIGH] ZimEye separately reported the same ZERA fuel-price figures: Diesel 50 at US$2.09 per litre and Blend E20 at US$2.08 per litre between 5 May and 15 May 2026.
- [HIGH] The Herald reported that the 17 April review reduced Blend E20 to US$2.08 from US$2.23 and diesel to US$2.09 from US$2.11.
- [HIGH] The Herald and Post On Sunday both reported ZERA saying government prioritised fuel security through market-sensitive pricing and tax reductions to cushion consumers and the economy.
- [HIGH] The Herald and Post On Sunday both reported ZERA saying the petrol reduction was helped by increased blending from E5 to E20.
- [HIGH] The Herald and Post On Sunday both reported that ZERA said Zimbabwe had fuel stocks covering more than three months and warned against panic buying or hoarding.
- [MEDIUM] ZERA's own petroleum overview states that fuel prices are market driven but monitored through a pricing formula accounting for associated costs.

## KEY QUOTES

- The Zimbabwean quoting ZERA: "Operators are advised to display the prices at their retail outlets at a prominent place in clearly legible letters. Please note that it is permissible to sell LPG at prices below the prescribed prices depending on one's trading advantages."
- The Herald quoting ZERA: "Since the last few months when volatility of fuel supply began, Government prioritised security of supplies through market sensitive pricing and reduction of taxes to cushion consumers and the economy at large."
- The Herald quoting ZERA: "The price of blend has also been reduced by the significant increase in the blending levels from E5 to E20."
- The Herald quoting ZERA: "Members of the public are advised not to engage in any panic buying or hoarding as the country is assured of adequate stocks for a period spanning over three months."

## KEY NUMBERS

- Diesel 50: US$2.09 per litre, The Zimbabwean and ZimEye.
- Blend E20: US$2.08 per litre, The Zimbabwean and ZimEye.
- Previous April petrol price before the 17 April cut: US$2.23 per litre, The Herald.
- Previous April diesel price before the 17 April cut: US$2.11 per litre, The Herald.

- LPG price: US$1.95 per kilogramme in May, The Zimbabwean and ZimEye, useful only as cost-of-living context.

## CONTEXT NEEDED FOR READER

The unchanged pump prices are relief, but not a full solution. Zimbabwe remains exposed to global fuel movements because it imports petroleum products. Government can cushion consumers through taxes, levies, blending and supply management, but repeated interventions create fiscal tradeoffs and may not hold if global oil rises again.

The story is slightly older than the scan label implied. Use cautious wording: say the latest verifiable notice found held prices flat for the 5 to 15 May window, and frame the article as analysis of what a price freeze means for motorists and inflation pressure.
