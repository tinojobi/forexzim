STORY: Africa's gold hub shifts to Zimbabwe as 2026 output booms
CLUSTER_ID: mining-gold-production-2026

KEY FACTS (with confidence tags):
- [HIGH] Total gold deliveries to Fidelity Gold Refinery (FGR) reached 12,636.51 kg between January and April 2026, 1.3% ahead of the same period in 2025 (12,471.6 kg) (Source: New Zimbabwe + Mining Zimbabwe + Equity Axis + TV BRICS + Business Insider Africa + Financial Gazette — all citing official FGR statistics)
- [HIGH] April 2026 large-scale mining deliveries rose 28.27% year-on-year to 1,213.93 kg, from 990.07 kg in April 2025 (Source: TV BRICS + Business Insider Africa + Equity Axis + Mining Zimbabwe)
- [HIGH] Total April 2026 deliveries were 3,324.59 kg, up 16.5% month-on-month from March's 2,854 kg, driven by ASM rebound (Source: Mining Zimbabwe + Financial Gazette + Equity Axis)
- [HIGH] 2025 full-year gold exports reached US$4.61 billion, accounting for ~47% of Zimbabwe's total exports (US$9.7 billion). Total deliveries: 46.73 tonnes, exceeding the 40-tonne government target (Source: Equity Axis + New Zimbabwe + Pindula News + TV BRICS + ZimStat)
- [HIGH] 2026 target is 50 tonnes (7% increase over 2025). FGR GM Peter Magaramombe confirmed optimism to Parliament's Public Accounts Committee (Source: Financial Gazette + Mining Zimbabwe + Equity Axis)
- [HIGH] ASM sector accounts for ~75% of national gold output; large-scale miners ~25%. In April 2026, ASM delivered 2,110.66 kg (up 20.7% MoM from March) while large-scale delivered 1,213.94 kg (Source: Mining Zimbabwe + Equity Axis)
- [HIGH] Gold prices in 2026 have averaged ~US$4,870/oz, with a high of US$5,400/oz in January. Current FGR buying price: ~US$4,230/oz (SG 90%+). The 10% royalty tier (above $5,000/oz) is now active (Source: exchange-rates.org + Mining Zimbabwe + Equity Axis)
- [HIGH] The ZiG currency system is underpinned by gold reserves. Gold export earnings are the primary source of foreign currency backing the ZiG (Source: Business Insider Africa + Equity Axis + RBZ)
- [MEDIUM] Annualised run rate from Jan-Apr 2026 implies ~37.9 tonnes, falling short of the 50-tonne target. Needs ~4,670 kg/month May-December to close the gap (Source: Equity Axis + Mining Zimbabwe)
- [MEDIUM] March 2026 deliveries dipped to 2,854 kg due to the RBZ's 90:10 ZiG retention policy, which disrupted ASM delivery confidence. Policy was subsequently suspended (Source: Equity Axis)
- [MEDIUM] New capacity coming online: Mazowe and Redwing mines reopening under Namib Minerals; expanded ASM formalisation at Elvington and Amaveni (Source: Mining Zimbabwe)

CONTEXT NEEDED FOR READER:
- Gold is Zimbabwe's single largest export earner, contributing ~47% of total exports and underpinning ZiG currency stability through forex inflows
- The 2026 target of 50 tonnes is ambitious; Jan-Apr run rate implies ~37.9 tonnes without acceleration
- The RBZ's 90:10 ZiG retention policy disrupted ASM deliveries in Q1 2026 but was suspended, restoring confidence
- Global gold prices above $5,000/oz trigger Zimbabwe's 10% royalty tier, boosting government revenue but increasing cost pressure on large-scale miners
- The ASM vs LSM dynamic: ASM dominates volume (75%) but large-scale miners are growing faster (28% YoY), suggesting structural shift
