STORY: OK Zimbabwe Suspends All Worker Salaries Indefinitely, Union Threatens Legal Action
CLUSTER_ID: wages-employment-labor

KEY FACTS (with confidence tags):
- [HIGH] OK Zimbabwe suspended all salaries and wages indefinitely from May 2026 via Joint Works Council resolution signed May 22, 2026 (Source: New Zimbabwe, Pindula, TechnoMag, iHarare, Breeze FM, Business Daily — all 6)
- [HIGH] CWUZ (Commercial Workers Union of Zimbabwe) condemned the move as "exploitative and illegal", saying they were never consulted (Source: New Zimbabwe — direct quotes from CWUZ Secretary General Cuthbert Chikwekwete)
- [HIGH] OK Zimbabwe entered corporate rescue Feb 24, 2026 under Section 122 of the Insolvency Act, appointing Bulisa Mbano of Grant Thornton as Corporate Rescue Practitioner (Source: Pindula, iHarare, TechnoMag, Business Daily, Breeze FM)
- [HIGH] Revenue collapsed from US$245M to US$40M (84% drop) in 11 months to Feb 2026; units sold fell from 208M to 32M (Source: Pindula, TechnoMag, Business Daily)
- [HIGH] Current liabilities US$38.7M vs current assets US$12.8M — massive deficit (Source: Pindula)
- [HIGH] 15 underperforming stores closed, 54 outlets still trading (Source: Pindula, Business Daily)
- [HIGH] CWUZ says the process violates Labour Act [Chapter 28:01] which requires 50% worker approval vote plus trade union consent (Source: New Zimbabwe — direct legal claim)
- [HIGH] CWUZ questions legitimacy of NEC chairman Givemore Dondo's signature, claiming his term expired (Source: New Zimbabwe)
- [MEDIUM] Over 3,000 employees affected (Source: TechnoMag, Business Daily)
- [MEDIUM] Revenue also fell from US$511M to US$245M in FY2025; EBITDA plunged from +US$40M to -US$5M (Source: Business Daily, TechnoMag)
- [MEDIUM] Store network shrank from 74 to 69; customer base fell from 34M to 22M; staff from 4,116 to 3,311 (Source: Business Daily — citing 2025 annual report)
- [MEDIUM] Workers still expected to commute and meet obligations without pay (Source: New Zimbabwe — Chikwekwete quote)

KEY QUOTES (attributed):
- "The arrangement is a direct violation of the Labour Act [Chapter 28:01]" - Cuthbert Chikwekwete, CWUZ Secretary General (Source: New Zimbabwe)
- "Even if he was still in office, his signature alone, outside due process, can never subject workers to this slave-like arrangement" - Cuthbert Chikwekwete (Source: New Zimbabwe)
- "Workers are still commuting to work. They have expenses to meet. They must pay their pension contributions at month-end, so how will that be possible?" - Cuthbert Chikwekwete (Source: New Zimbabwe)
- "The first port of call is to come up with a rescue plan, which must be approved by the affected parties" - Cuthbert Chikwekwete (Source: New Zimbabwe)
- "This is a difficult and uncomfortable measure but a necessary intervention in our efforts to turn around the business" - Joint Works Council resolution (Source: Pindula, iHarare, Breeze FM)

CONTEXT NEEDED FOR READER:
- OK Zimbabwe is one of Zimbabwe's oldest and largest supermarket chains (founded 1942), operating OK Stores, Bon Marché, and OKmart brands
- Corporate rescue under Section 122 Insolvency Act is a legal protection mechanism to restructure without creditor litigation
- CWUZ is the primary trade union representing commercial/retail workers in Zimbabwe
- The Labour Act [Chapter 28:01] governs collective bargaining and requires worker consultation
