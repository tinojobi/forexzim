# Research Brief

## STORY
Zimbabwe says Huayou plans a lithium carbonate plant, extending the country's push from concentrate exports toward higher-value battery materials.

## PRIMARY KEYWORD
Zimbabwe lithium carbonate plant

## CLUSTER ID
2026-06-06-huayou-lithium-carbonate-plant

## KEY FACTS
- [HIGH] Bloomberg via MINING.com reported on June 4 that Mines Minister Polite Kambamura said Zhejiang Huayou Cobalt plans to produce lithium carbonate in Zimbabwe.
- [HIGH] Reuters reported on April 28 that Huayou had already shipped Africa's first consignment of lithium sulphate from Zimbabwe.
- [HIGH] Reuters reported the Huayou lithium sulphate plant cost about US$400 million, was completed in October 2025, and has annual capacity of 50,000 metric tons.
- [HIGH] Reuters reported on April 8 that Zimbabwe resumed some lithium concentrate exports only under quotas and requires written commitments on lithium sulphate plants before January 1, 2027.
- [HIGH] Reuters reported Zimbabwe plans a full ban on lithium concentrate exports from January 1, 2027.
- [HIGH] MINING.com, citing Bloomberg, said Zimbabwe accounted for about 10% of global mined lithium production last year, citing USGS.
- [MEDIUM] MINING.com, citing Bloomberg, said Zimbabwe's mining sector could generate as much as US$7 billion this year, with US$2 billion already recorded in the first half.

## KEY QUOTES
- "They will be producing lithium carbonate, which is more valued," Mines Minister Polite Kambamura, via Bloomberg/MINING.com.
- Reuters: Huayou's first lithium sulphate shipment was described as the first lithium salt export from Zimbabwe and Africa.

## KEY NUMBERS
- US$400 million, cost of Huayou's lithium sulphate plant
- 50,000 metric tons, annual sulphate capacity
- 10%, export tax on lithium concentrate until the 2027 ban
- January 1, 2027, start of full lithium concentrate export ban
- 1.13 million metric tons, Zimbabwe spodumene exports to China in 2025
- About 15%, share of China's lithium concentrate imports in 2025 supplied by Zimbabwe
- About 10%, Zimbabwe share of global mined lithium output last year

## CONTEXT NEEDED FOR READER
- Lithium sulphate is an intermediate product. Lithium carbonate is a higher-value battery chemical further up the value chain.
- Zimbabwe is trying to capture more value locally instead of exporting mainly raw or semi-processed material.
- The policy direction is not theoretical: export suspensions, quotas, taxes, and a 2027 ban are already on the books.
- The key caution is that the carbonate plant is still a plan, not a completed facility.
