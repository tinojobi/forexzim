KEY FACTS (with confidence tags):
- [HIGH] Zimbabwe operates a multi-tier exchange rate system: RBZ interbank rate, commercial bank rates, and parallel market rates. Each serves different segments of the market. (Source: RBZ Monetary Policy Statement + multiple bank rate circulars)
- [HIGH] The RBZ publishes a daily interbank mid-rate. As of May 29, 2026: USD/ZWG buy 26.24, sell 27.59. (Source: RBZ daily exchange rate PDFs)
- [HIGH] Commercial banks set their own rates within a band around the interbank rate. Stanbic Bank (May 29): buy 25.98, sell 27.86. Rates vary between banks by 1-3%. (Source: Stanbic Bank exchange rate circular)
- [HIGH] Banks differentiate between TT (telegraphic transfer) rates for electronic transfers and cash rates for physical currency. Cash rates typically carry a premium. (Source: FBC Bank, Stanbic Bank rate circulars)
- [HIGH] The spread between buy and sell rates is how banks profit from forex transactions. The spread is typically 5-10% depending on the currency and bank. (Source: FBC Bank rate tables)
- [HIGH] The parallel market rate (street rate) runs 10-20% above the official interbank rate. As of late May 2026: informal rate ~30-33 ZiG per USD, cash rate ~40 ZiG per USD. (Source: ZimPriceCheck)
- [HIGH] 19 banking institutions operate in Zimbabwe: 14 commercial banks, 4 building societies, and POSB. Each publishes its own rates. (Source: RBZ Banking Sector Architecture)
- [MEDIUM] The RBZ Bank Policy Rate was raised to 35% in September 2024 to curb inflation and exchange rate pressures. This affects lending rates but not directly the retail exchange rate. (Source: RBZ MPS February 2025)
- [MEDIUM] The exchange rate premium between official and parallel rates has been contained since October 2024 following tighter monetary policy. (Source: RBZ MPS February 2025)
