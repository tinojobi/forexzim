# Research Brief: World Bank lifts Zimbabwe 2026 growth forecast to 5%

## KEY FACTS

- [HIGH] The World Bank's January 2026 Global Economic Prospects country forecast table shows Zimbabwe GDP growth at 6.6% for 2025, 5.0% for 2026 and 5.0% for 2027. Confirmed by World Bank GEP material and NewZimbabwe.
- [HIGH] The World Bank's Sub-Saharan Africa analysis says Zimbabwe's 2025 growth rose sharply because of agricultural recovery and investment in extractive sectors including gold, lithium, iron and steel. Confirmed by World Bank GEP analysis and NewZimbabwe.
- [HIGH] The World Bank's Zimbabwe Economic Update projects 2025 GDP growth of 6.6% and medium-term growth at 5% in 2026, while warning about fiscal slippages, external shocks and climate-related disasters.
- [HIGH] The Zimbabwe Independent reported the 2026 budget framework at about US$9.5 billion in spending against US$9.4 billion in expected revenue, with an almost balanced budget and a small deficit of US$105.9 million.
- [MEDIUM] NewZimbabwe reported the government is targeting a budget deficit around 0.2% of GDP in 2026.
- [HIGH] The World Bank Zimbabwe Economic Update says tight monetary policy since late 2024 helped improve inflation dynamics and stabilise the ZiG, with inflation expected to moderate to single digits in 2026.

## KEY QUOTES

- World Bank GEP analysis: "In Zimbabwe, growth increased sharply in 2025 to 6.6 percent, owing to a recovery in agricultural production, and investments in extractive sectors (gold, lithium, iron, and steel), which boosted industrial output."
- Zimbabwe Independent quoting Mthuli Ncube: "In 2026, economic growth is projected at 5%."
- World Bank Zimbabwe Economic Update, Victor Steenbergen: "Now that the macroeconomy is improving, the Government's position in re-prioritizing efforts to improve the ease of doing business ... are more than necessary."

## KEY NUMBERS

- 2025 GDP growth estimate: 6.6%, World Bank GEP and Zimbabwe Economic Update.
- 2026 GDP growth forecast: 5.0%, World Bank GEP and Zimbabwe Economic Update.
- 2027 GDP growth forecast: 5.0%, World Bank GEP.
- Previous 2026 forecast cited by NewZimbabwe: 4.6%.
- Budget spending: US$9.5 billion, Zimbabwe Independent.
- Expected revenue: US$9.4 billion, Zimbabwe Independent.
- Projected deficit: US$105.9 million and roughly 0.2% of GDP, Zimbabwe Independent and NewZimbabwe.
- Sub-Saharan Africa growth forecast: 4.3% in 2026 and 4.5% in 2027, World Bank regional highlights.

## CONTEXT NEEDED FOR READER

Zimbabwe's 5% forecast is not just a headline number. It depends on agriculture holding after recovery from weather shocks, mining investment continuing, commodity prices staying supportive and fiscal policy staying tight enough to protect currency and inflation gains. The budget angle matters because a near-balanced fiscal target gives the government less room for unfunded spending.
