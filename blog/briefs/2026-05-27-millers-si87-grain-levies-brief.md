STORY: Millers demand repeal of unpublished SI imposing more tax on imported flour, maize
CLUSTER_ID: tax-fiscal-policy-trade

KEY FACTS (with confidence tags):
- [HIGH] SI 87 of 2025 imposes levies on imported grain: wheat US$89.25/MT, maize US$40/MT, soya US$20/MT, soya meal US$35/MT (Source: New Zimbabwe, ZimEye, 263Chat, Equity Axis, Herald — all 5)
- [HIGH] GMAZ demands repeal, calling SI unconstitutional — Section 298(2) limits Minister to levies on locally grown produce only (Source: New Zimbabwe, ZimEye)
- [HIGH] Price projections: 10kg roller meal US$4.60→US$5.20 (13%), 50kg bakers flour US$36→US$41 (14%), bread US$1→US$1.15 (15%), stockfeeds +18% (Source: New Zimbabwe — GMAZ letter)
- [HIGH] SI requires 40% local sourcing by April 2026, 100% by April 2028 (Source: Herald, Equity Axis, 263Chat, ZimEye)
- [HIGH] Levies fund the Agricultural Revolving Fund for irrigation infrastructure (Source: 263Chat, ZimEye, Equity Axis)
- [HIGH] No stakeholder consultation occurred before levies imposed — Agricultural Marketing Act requires it (Source: New Zimbabwe — GMAZ claim)
- [HIGH] El Niño 2026/27 food security fears driving policy — successive droughts reduced local output (Source: ZimEye, 263Chat)
- [MEDIUM] Cabinet announced no new levies just two weeks before SI was introduced (Source: New Zimbabwe — GMAZ claim)
- [MEDIUM] Farmer organisations endorse levies, arguing they strengthen local markets (Source: 263Chat)
- [MEDIUM] Maize levy applies for 90 days; soya/soya meal until 31 August 2026 (Source: 263Chat)

KEY QUOTES (attributed):
- "The levies are unprecedented as they were never imposed since 1890 or in recorded modern history." - GMAZ letter (Source: New Zimbabwe)
- "The Minister is limited to impose a levy on locally grown agricultural produce only. Therefore, the levy on imported agricultural is deemed to be a tax. The Minister has acted outside his province." - GMAZ letter (Source: New Zimbabwe)
- "The Agricultural Marketing Act decrees that levies must be imposed after consultation with all key stakeholders, such as ourselves. The consultations were not done." - GMAZ letter (Source: New Zimbabwe)

CONTEXT NEEDED FOR READER:
- SI 87 of 2025 was gazetted in September 2025 under the Agricultural Marketing Authority Act
- Zimbabwe has faced successive droughts, reducing maize and oilseed output and triggering large-scale imports
- The Agricultural Revolving Fund is meant to finance irrigation and support local farmers
- GMAZ represents the majority of grain millers in Zimbabwe
- The SI's 40% local sourcing deadline (April 2026) has already passed or is imminent
