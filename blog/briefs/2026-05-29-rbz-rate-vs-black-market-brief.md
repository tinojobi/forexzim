KEY FACTS (with confidence tags):
- [HIGH] Zimbabwe operates a dual exchange rate: the RBZ interbank rate (official) and the parallel market rate (informal/black market). The gap between them is called the exchange rate premium. (Source: RBZ Quarterly Snapshot Q1 2026 + CZI + Financial Gazette)
- [HIGH] The RBZ interbank rate averaged ZiG25.59 per USD in Q1 2026. The parallel market premium remained below 20% during the same period. (Source: RBZ Quarterly Snapshot Q1 2026)
- [HIGH] The parallel market premium was previously over 100%. Tighter monetary policy since April 2024 has narrowed it significantly. (Source: CZI via Herald)
- [HIGH] In April 2026, CZI reported the premium widened from below 20% to just under 25% as businesses turned to the parallel market for immediate forex needs. (Source: Zimbabwe Independent + CZI)
- [HIGH] Cash USD trades at a further premium: ~ZiG40 per USD for physical cash, vs ~ZiG30-33 for informal electronic rates. (Source: ZimPriceCheck)
- [HIGH] The RBZ has injected US$1.6 billion into the forex market since April 2024 to support exchange rate stability under the Willing-Buyer Willing-Seller (WBWS) arrangement. (Source: RBZ + Financial Gazette)
- [HIGH] ZiG annual inflation fell to 4.1% in January 2026, the first single-digit rate in three decades. (Source: CZI via Herald + RBZ)
- [HIGH] Foreign currency reserves stood at US$1.4 billion (1.5 months import cover) as at March 2026, providing about 6x cover of ZiG reserve money. (Source: RBZ Q1 2026 Snapshot)
- [MEDIUM] Government plans to phase out the US dollar and establish a unified ZiG-only currency by 2030, contingent on meeting key benchmarks. (Source: Herald via CZI report)
- [MEDIUM] Access to forex through official channels remains constrained for some businesses, forcing reliance on the parallel market for imports and urgent payments. (Source: Zimbabwe Independent + CZI)
