STORY: Fidelity Gold Refinery intensifies drive to formalise small-scale gold mining sector, plug leakages
CLUSTER_ID: fidelity-formalise-asm-gold-leakages-may-2026

KEY FACTS (with confidence tags):
- [HIGH] FGR General Manager Peter Magaramombe says formalisation will ensure all gold purchased originates from legitimate, traceable sources (Source: New Zimbabwe May 13)
- [HIGH] ~US$2 billion lost annually through gold smuggling, primarily from informal miners (Source: New Zimbabwe May 13 + WeMineZimbabwe + ZimEye May 2025)
- [HIGH] Root causes: poor recovery techniques, unmonitored production, black-market pricing, syndicates (Source: New Zimbabwe May 13)
- [HIGH] Small-scale miners contribute ~75% of gold deliveries to FGR (Source: WeMineZimbabwe + Zimbabwe Situation + Equity Axis)
- [HIGH] 2025 gold output: 46.7 tonnes, record high (Source: New Zimbabwe + multiple sources)
- [HIGH] 2026 target: 50 tonnes (Source: New Zimbabwe May 13 + WeMineZimbabwe)
- [MEDIUM] 22 tonnes of gold smuggled annually worth US$2 billion+ (Source: WeMineZimbabwe)
- [MEDIUM] Gold exports surged 135.6% in January 2026 to US$290.1 million (Source: Zimbabwe Situation)
- [MEDIUM] 2025 gold earnings: US$4.48 billion (Source: Zimbabwe Situation + Equity Axis)
- [MEDIUM] Small-scale miners pay 1-2% royalty rate vs 5% for large-scale (Source: Equity Axis)
- [MEDIUM] 90:10 retention policy (90% forex, 10% ZiG) was suspended due to implementation challenges (Source: Zimbabwe Situation)
- [LOW] Some large-scale miners allegedly channel output through small-scale channels to avoid higher royalties (Source: Equity Axis, unverified)

KEY QUOTES (attributed):
- "We need to formalise the artisanal and small-scale miners so that every day we know exactly where we are, where the gold is coming from and where it is going." - Peter Magaramombe, FGR General Manager (New Zimbabwe May 13)
- "I can assure you, honourable members, we will achieve this target of 50 tonnes for this year." - Peter Magaramombe (New Zimbabwe May 13)

CONTEXT NEEDED FOR READER:
- Zimbabwe's gold sector is split between large-scale miners (LSM) and artisanal/small-scale miners (ASM)
- ASM dominates deliveries but operates largely informally
- Gold is Zimbabwe's top export and the main backing for the ZiG currency
- FGR is the sole legal gold buyer, refiner, and exporter
- The formalisation push follows RBZ's suspended 90:10 forex retention policy for small-scale miners
- Gold smuggling deprives the state of forex earnings needed for ZiG backing and import cover
