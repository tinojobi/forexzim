# Zimbabwe Export Ban Drives Lithium and PGMs to Nearly $1 Billion in Q1 Mineral Sales

**Research Brief:**
- STORY: Zimbabwe export ban drives lithium and PGMs to nearly $1 billion mineral sales
- CLUSTER_ID: mmcz-q1-2026-mineral-sales
- PRIMARY KEYWORD: Zimbabwe mineral sales
- TARGET WORD COUNT: 500-800

KEY FACTS (with confidence tags):
- [HIGH] MMCZ Q1 2026 total mineral sales: 1,288,761 tonnes valued at US$983.85M, up 79% YoY in value and 27% in volume (zimbabwenow.co.zw + Africa Business Insider)
- [HIGH] PGMs contributed US$543.97M — largest revenue segment. PGM concentrate sales surged 98% in volume, 319% in value YoY (zimbabwenow.co.zw)
- [HIGH] Lithium sales: 240,826 tonnes valued at US$178.64M, 106% value increase despite only 2% volume growth (zimbabwenow.co.zw)
- [HIGH] Zimbabwe supplies ~15% of spodumene imported into China (zimbabwenow.co.zw + Fastmarkets + SMM)
- [HIGH] Export ban imposed Feb 25, 2026 — bans all raw minerals and lithium concentrates (Reuters)
- [HIGH] Ban accelerated from original 2027 timeline (Reuters + Fastmarkets)
- [MEDIUM] MMCZ projected US$3.5B annual revenue for 2026 (zimbabwenow.co.zw)
- [MEDIUM] Diamond exports under pressure from lab-grown competition (zimbabwenow.co.zw)
- [MEDIUM] MMCZ absorbing ~US$462,292/month in lost commission from lithium ban (Zimbabwe Independent)
- [MEDIUM] Huayou shipped Africa's first lithium sulphate from Arcadia mine — $400M plant, 50,000t annual capacity (Prospect Intel)
- [MEDIUM] Sinomine planning $500M lithium sulphate plant at Bikita (Prospect Intel)
- [LOW] Industry estimates suggest Zimbabwe could lose ~US$60M monthly in royalties and taxes from ban (Zimbabwe Independent, unattributed source)

SOURCES: zimbabwenow.co.zw, Africa Business Insider, Reuters, Equity Axis, Zimbabwe Independent, Prospect Intel, Fastmarkets

ANGLE: The Feb 2026 export ban is producing tangible results — Q1 mineral sales hit $983.85M, up 79% YoY. Beneficiation is driving value growth even where volumes are flat.
