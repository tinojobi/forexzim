# Research Brief: ZiG credit facility stalls while banks push for lending revival

## KEY FACTS

- [HIGH] The RBZ Targeted Finance Facility was expanded to ZiG1.2 billion after an additional ZiG600 million allocation, with the facility intended to support productive sectors. Sources: Business Times, NewsDay.
- [HIGH] NewsDay reported that only ZiG56.9 million of the ZiG1.2 billion facility had been drawn down by April 15, 2026, less than 5% of available funds. Sources: NewsDay, Business Daily.
- [HIGH] Business Daily reported that bankers say the RBZ's tight policy helped restore exchange-rate stability and reduce inflation, but is now constraining credit growth. Sources: Business Daily, Kwedu News.
- [HIGH] Business Daily reported the policy rate is around 35%, while the TFF is priced around 30% per year, implying lending rates around 35% to 40% after bank margins. Sources: Business Daily, Kwedu News.
- [MEDIUM] NewsDay reported business-facing lending rates of 40% to 47%, citing the Zimbabwe National Chamber of Commerce. Source: NewsDay.
- [MEDIUM] NewsDay reported that banks lend about 44 cents per deposit dollar, with the balance held in government securities, treasury bills and liquid assets. Source: NewsDay.
- [MEDIUM] NewsDay reported economist Chenai Mutambasere saying more than 80% of available liquidity is concentrated in a few banks, leaving SMEs locked out. Source: NewsDay.

## KEY QUOTES

- John Mushayavanhu, RBZ Governor, described low TFF uptake as disappointing, according to NewsDay and Business Daily.
- Business Daily quoted one senior banker asking what kind of business can generate a 40% annual margin when prices are not increasing.
- Bankers Association of Zimbabwe CEO Fanwell Mutogo said lending remains demand-driven and depends on viable applications, according to Business Daily and Kwedu News.

## KEY NUMBERS

- Facility size: ZiG1.2 billion.
- Drawn down by April 15, 2026: ZiG56.9 million.
- Share used: less than 5%.
- RBZ policy rate: around 35%.
- TFF pricing: around 30% per year.
- Expected lending after margins: around 35% to 40%.
- Reported commercial lending range: 40% to 47%.
- Approximate unused credit equivalent in NewsDay calculation: about US$45 million at US$1 to ZiG25.2.

## CONTEXT NEEDED FOR READER

Zimbabwe has regained a measure of price and exchange-rate stability after tight monetary policy, but the same tight policy may now be blocking credit transmission. The issue is not just whether money exists in the banking system. It is whether SMEs and productive sectors can borrow at rates that make commercial sense.
