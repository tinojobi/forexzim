# Research Brief: Zimbabwe Black Market Rate Today

## KEY FACTS

- [HIGH] Zimbabwe's official exchange rate is published by the Reserve Bank of Zimbabwe as an interbank rate, with daily PDFs listed on the RBZ exchange rates page. Source: RBZ exchange rates page.
- [HIGH] RBZ policy material says ZiG stability depends on liquidity control, market-based instruments, foreign reserves management, and the willing-buyer willing-seller interbank market. Source: RBZ Monetary Policy Statements.
- [HIGH] RBZ clarified, as reported by Pindula, that the market-determined rate refers to the interbank foreign exchange market and is determined by authorised dealers, meaning banks, under the willing-buyer willing-seller system. Source: Pindula quoting RBZ Governor John Mushayavanhu.
- [MEDIUM] Informal or black market rate trackers show a different cash/informal rate from the official interbank rate. Zimpricecheck's May 2026 example listed 1 USD to ZiG official at 25.34 ZWG, cash at 40.00 ZWG, and informal sector range at 30.00 to 33.00 ZWG. Source: Zimpricecheck.
- [HIGH] ZiG's formal ISO-style currency code is ZWG after RBZ announced approval of the code. Source: RBZ press statement page and NewZimbabwe reporting.

## KEY QUOTES

- Pindula quotes RBZ saying the exchange rate is determined in the interbank market for foreign exchange under the willing-buyer willing-seller arrangement.
- RBZ Monetary Policy Statements page says the central bank will deepen the WBWS interbank market to improve price discovery and market efficiency.

## KEY NUMBERS

- Zimpricecheck example: official 1 USD to ZiG rate, 25.34 ZWG; cash rate, 40.00 ZWG; informal range, 30.00 to 33.00 ZWG.
- ZiG/ZWG currency code transition effective in 2024 per RBZ/NewZimbabwe reporting.

## CONTEXT NEEDED FOR READER

Readers searching for "black market rate today" usually want a practical explanation, not encouragement to trade illegally. The article should explain what the rate means, why it differs from official/bank rates, why it changes quickly, and when to use official tools instead.
