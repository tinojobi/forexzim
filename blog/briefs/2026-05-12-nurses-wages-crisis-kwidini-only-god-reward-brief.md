# Research Brief: Nurses' Wages Crisis — Kwidini Says "Only God Can Reward You"

STORY: Nurses' Wages Crisis: Deputy Minister Says "Only God Can Reward You" as Healthcare Workers Demand US$600-800
CLUSTER_ID: nurses-wages-crisis-kwidini-may-2026

## KEY FACTS (with confidence tags):

- [HIGH] Deputy Health Minister Sleiman Kwidini told nurses "only God can reward a nurse" during International Nurses Day commemorations on May 12, 2026 (Source: iHarare, May 13)
- [HIGH] Nurses are demanding US$600-800/month salaries (Source: iHarare + New Zimbabwe + NewsDay)
- [HIGH] Some public nurses currently earn below US$300/month; base salary around US$240 + ZiG component worth less than US$100 (Source: iHarare + NewsDay)
- [HIGH] Kwidini: "It doesn't make sense to break the bank for nurses, yet we do not have enough money" (Source: iHarare, May 13)
- [HIGH] Kwidini acknowledged nurses' welfare concerns but said government resources are limited; promised "gradual improvement" (Source: iHarare, May 13)
- [HIGH] ZINA reported nurse-to-patient ratios stretched to 1:20 or even 1:30, far above safe standards (Source: New Zimbabwe, May 12)
- [HIGH] Over 4,000 nurses applied for government verification letters to seek jobs abroad (Source: New Zimbabwe, Nov 2025)
- [HIGH] Zimbabwe faces shortfall of at least 14,000 health workers (Source: DW)
- [HIGH] An estimated 7,000+ nurses have emigrated; 3,600+ registered in UK alone (Source: DW + Context)
- [HIGH] UK nurse salary: ~GBP 3,150/month (~US$4,150) vs Zimbabwe ~US$250/month (Source: DW)
- [HIGH] Nurses rejected US$30-40 pay rise in April 2026 as "insulting" and "a mere token" (Source: iHarare, April 15)
- [HIGH] 3-day nationwide strike occurred April 20-22, 2026 disrupting services at major hospitals (Source: iHarare + NewsDay)
- [HIGH] Government renovated Adlum House at Parirenyatwa for student nurses; Kwidini defended this amid social media criticism (Source: iHarare, May 13)
- [HIGH] Some nurses walk 8km+ to work due to soaring transport costs (Source: NewsDay, March 24)
- [MEDIUM] WHO added Zimbabwe to "red list" of countries with health worker shortages (Source: Context/TRF)
- [MEDIUM] Government accused of withholding verification letters to stem brain drain (Source: iHarare + Context)
- [HIGH] ZINA president Enock Dongo: expecting nurses to survive on current pay is "purely a mockery" (Source: NewsDay, March 24)

## KEY QUOTES:

- "We were laughing the other day, discussing the industrial action by public health institution nurses. We asked ourselves if there is anyone in this world with the capacity to adequately pay a nurse. A nurse cannot be paid by anyone, and only God can reward a nurse." — Sleiman Kwidini, Deputy Health Minister (iHarare, May 13)

- "It doesn't make sense to break the bank for nurses, yet we do not have enough money, that would be a lie, but your welfare will be gradually improved." — Sleiman Kwidini (iHarare, May 13)

- "Nurses are the first port of call for all clients visiting hospitals. Yet they are blamed for systemic resource shortages, while officials seem focused on workshops and lining pockets instead of delivering services." — Enock Dongo, ZINA President (NewsDay, March 24)

- "The salaries are so inconsequential to such an extent that they remain a mere token that cannot be taken seriously." — ZINA statement (iHarare, April 15)
