# Research Brief: Why Fiscal Discipline Matters for ZiG Stability in 2026

## Story trigger
Herald and Zimbabwe Mail reported that economists are urging Zimbabwe to maintain strict fiscal and monetary discipline to preserve recent stability gains and build confidence in the ZiG.

## Verification status
Ready to draft with cautious framing. The exact "experts say" angle is media-reported, but the underlying policy case is corroborated by official IMF guidance and World Bank fiscal analysis.

## KEY FACTS
- [HIGH] The IMF approved a 10 month Staff Monitored Program for Zimbabwe on 16 April 2026 aimed at consolidating stabilization gains, improving budget execution, strengthening expenditure controls, and sustaining monetary discipline. Source: IMF press release, 16 Apr 2026.
- [HIGH] The IMF said Zimbabwe's inflation declined to 4.4 percent in March 2026 and linked the improvement to a stable foreign exchange rate and tight monetary conditions. Source: IMF press release, 16 Apr 2026.
- [HIGH] The IMF said the program supports prudent budget execution based on a conservative revenue outlook to avoid new domestic arrears. Source: IMF press release, 16 Apr 2026.
- [HIGH] The IMF staff level agreement of 6 Feb 2026 said inflation fell to 4.1 percent in January 2026, growth in 2026 was projected at about 5 percent, and the primary fiscal balance was expected to post a surplus of about 0.5 percent of GDP. Source: IMF press release, 6 Feb 2026.
- [HIGH] The World Bank said fiscal policy can be a critical anchor for Zimbabwe's macroeconomic stability and that exchange rate and monetary distortions caused more than US$4.5 billion in treasury losses between 2020 and 2023. Source: World Bank Public Finance Review note, 26 Feb 2025.
- [MEDIUM] The Herald reported economists including Gift Mugano and Tony Chipanga urging zero tolerance for quasi fiscal activities, transparent cash based budgeting, better reserve disclosure, and restrictive data driven policy until single digit inflation is sustained. Source: Herald, 7 Jun 2026.
- [MEDIUM] Reuters reported around 80 to 85 percent of transactions in Zimbabwe are conducted in foreign currency, underscoring the credibility challenge for the ZiG. Source: Reuters, 8 Apr 2024.

## KEY QUOTES
- IMF: "The program emphasizes prudent budget execution, improved cash and expenditure controls, sustained monetary discipline, and governance reforms to enhance transparency and manage fiscal risks."
- IMF: "Zimbabwe's economic recovery continues, supported by tight monetary policy, improving fiscal discipline, and favorable external conditions."
- World Bank: "Fiscal policy can be a critical anchor for Zimbabwe's macroeconomic stability going forward."
- Herald via Gift Mugano: authorities should maintain restrictive, data driven policy and enforce transparent budgeting with zero tolerance for quasi fiscal activity.

## CONTEXT NEEDED FOR READER
- Zimbabwe's recent calm is policy driven, not yet deeply trusted by households and firms.
- Fiscal discipline matters because off budget spending, arrears, or quasi fiscal activity can quickly spill into inflation and exchange rate pressure.
- Monetary discipline alone is not enough if public finances drift or if confidence in reserve backing remains weak.
- The ZiG still operates in an economy where the US dollar dominates day to day trade.
