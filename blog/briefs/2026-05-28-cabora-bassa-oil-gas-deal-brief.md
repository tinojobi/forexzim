# Research Brief: Zimbabwe Signs Landmark Oil and Gas Deal for Cabora Bassa Project
**Date:** May 28, 2026 | **Primary Keyword:** Cabora Bassa oil and gas deal
**Sources:** iHarare, Reuters (via MarketScreener), SmallCaps, Mining Zimbabwe

## KEY FACTS

**[HIGH] Petroleum Production Sharing Agreement (PPSA) signed May 27, 2026**
- Between Zimbabwe government and Geo Associates (Invictus Energy 80% owned, Mutapa Investment Fund 20%)
- Signed under Special Grant 4571 in Harare
- Covers 360,000 hectares in Cabora Bassa Basin (Muzarabani/Mbire districts, northern Zimbabwe)
- Confirmed by: iHarare, Reuters, SmallCaps, Mining Zimbabwe

**[HIGH] Mukuyu discovery (2023) is globally significant**
- ~230 million barrels oil equivalent
- ~1.3 trillion cubic feet (tcf) of gas
- Among most significant Sub-Saharan African discoveries in recent years
- Confirmed by: iHarare, Reuters, SmallCaps

**[HIGH] Next well: Musuma-1, targeting H2 2026**
- Targets: 1.2 tcf gas + 73 million barrels condensate
- $10 million raised in April 2026 to fund drilling
- Invictus has invested over $100 million since 2022
- Environmental Impact Assessment renewed through March 2027
- Confirmed by: iHarare, SmallCaps, Mining Zimbabwe

**[HIGH] Expected economic impact**
- Mthuli Ncube: reduce Zimbabwe's fuel import dependency, ease foreign currency demand
- Improved balance of payments
- Pilot gas-to-power projects possible within 12-18 months
- Confirmed by: iHarare, Reuters

**[MEDIUM] PPSA is a hybrid model**
- State can choose between profit share or gas share
- Serves as template for all future Zimbabwe petroleum agreements
- Confirmed by: Reuters, Mining Zimbabwe

**[MEDIUM] Long-term potential up to 20 tcf of gas**
- Mining Zimbabwe cited estimates up to 20 tcf
- Not yet independently verified by other sources

## CONTEXT NEEDED FOR READER
- Zimbabwe imports virtually all its petroleum — massive forex drain
- Cabora Bassa has been explored since the 1990s (Mobil originally)
- This is the first PPSA in Zimbabwe's history — no existing framework existed
- Invictus Energy is ASX-listed (IVZ), publicly traded
- The project could transform Zimbabwe from energy importer to potential exporter
