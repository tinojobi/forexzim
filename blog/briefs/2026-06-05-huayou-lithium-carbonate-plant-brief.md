# Research Brief: Huayou lithium carbonate plant in Zimbabwe

## Story cluster
- Headline under review: Zimbabwe to host new lithium carbonate plant
- Date: 2026-06-05
- Primary company: Zhejiang Huayou Cobalt / Prospect Lithium Zimbabwe
- Core policy theme: lithium beneficiation and local value addition

## KEY FACTS
- [HIGH] Bloomberg/MINING.com reports that Mines Minister Polite Kambamura said Huayou plans a lithium carbonate plant in Zimbabwe. Source: MINING.com/Bloomberg, 2026-06-04.
- [HIGH] Reuters reported on 2026-04-28 that Huayou had already shipped Africa's first consignment of lithium sulphate from Zimbabwe. Source: Reuters.
- [HIGH] Reuters reported Huayou completed a US$400 million Zimbabwe processing plant in October 2025 with capacity around 50,000 metric tons per year of lithium sulphate. Source: Reuters, 2026-04-28.
- [HIGH] The Herald reported Prospect Lithium Zimbabwe exported Zimbabwe's first batch of lithium sulphate in April 2026 from Arcadia in Goromonzi. Source: The Herald, 2026-04-28.
- [HIGH] Pindula reported the same first-export milestone and described lithium sulphate as an intermediate product that can be refined into lithium carbonate or lithium hydroxide. Source: Pindula, 2026-04-29.
- [HIGH] Zimbabwe suspended lithium concentrate exports in February 2026 and plans a full ban from January 2027 as part of its beneficiation push. Source: Reuters, 2026-02-25 and 2026-04-28; The Herald, 2026-04-28.
- [MEDIUM] Ecofin says lithium carbonate commands materially higher value than concentrate or sulphate and frames the Huayou plan as a step further up the value chain. Source: Ecofin, 2026-06-05.
- [MEDIUM] Zimbabwe accounted for roughly 10% of global mined lithium output last year, according to the minister as cited by Bloomberg/MINING.com. Source: MINING.com/Bloomberg, 2026-06-04.
- [MEDIUM] Lithium sector export revenue for Zimbabwe was about US$571 million in 2025. Source: Ecofin, 2026-06-05.

## KEY QUOTES
- [HIGH] "They will be producing lithium carbonate, which is more valued." — Mines Minister Polite Kambamura, via Bloomberg/MINING.com.
- [HIGH] "This inaugural shipment represents the first lithium salt ever produced in Zimbabwe and across Africa." — Huayou Zimbabwe unit, via Reuters.
- [HIGH] "We can only open this after January 1, 2027. For now, we are issuing export quotas to avoid disruptions while beneficiation capacity is being developed." — Kambamura, via The Herald.

## KEY NUMBERS
- US$400 million: Huayou processing plant investment. [HIGH]
- 50,000 metric tons/year: Reuters-stated annual lithium sulphate capacity. [HIGH]
- 80,000 metric tons/year: The Herald-stated planned annual lithium sulphate output. [MEDIUM, conflicts with Reuters/Pindula figure]
- US$571 million: Zimbabwe lithium export revenue in 2025. [MEDIUM]
- ~10%: Zimbabwe share of global mined lithium output last year, per Bloomberg-cited minister remarks. [MEDIUM]
- January 2027: planned full ban on lithium concentrate exports. [HIGH]

## CONTEXT NEEDED FOR READER
- This is not Zimbabwe's first lithium processing story. The more solid milestone already happened in April 2026: first lithium sulphate exports.
- The new angle is the possible move from sulphate into lithium carbonate, which is a higher-value battery chemical.
- The story still lacks company-side detail: no investment size for the carbonate expansion, no timeline, and no technical explanation from Huayou itself.
- There is a terminology trap here: older reporting refers to lithium carbonate equivalent, lithium sulphate, and lithium carbonate separately. The draft must distinguish them cleanly.

## DRAFTING RECOMMENDATION
- Ready for drafting, but only if framed carefully as a planned next step, not a commissioned plant.
- Best angle: Zimbabwe is trying to move from shipping lithium concentrate, then lithium sulphate, toward higher-value lithium carbonate under the beneficiation policy.
- Main caveat to state clearly: the carbonate plan is currently attributed to the mines minister/Bloomberg reporting, with limited project specifics from Huayou.
