# Research Brief: Companies intensify hunt for black market dollar as shortages deepen

## KEY FACTS

- [MEDIUM] Zimbabwe Independent reports that CZI's latest report says manufacturers have intensified demand for US dollars on the parallel market as official access remains constrained. Source: https://www.theindependent.co.zw/local-news/article/200055817/companies-intensify-hunt-for-black-market-dollar-as-shortages-deepen
- [MEDIUM] CZI figures reported by Zimbabwe Independent say ZiG appreciated by about 0.9% on the official market between March and April, but lost roughly 4.2% on the parallel market. Source: https://www.theindependent.co.zw/local-news/article/200055817/companies-intensify-hunt-for-black-market-dollar-as-shortages-deepen
- [MEDIUM] The reported official/parallel premium widened from below 20% in March to nearly 25% in April. Source: https://www.theindependent.co.zw/local-news/article/200055817/companies-intensify-hunt-for-black-market-dollar-as-shortages-deepen
- [HIGH] NewsDay separately reported fuel stations refusing ZiG because fuel is sourced in US dollars and official forex conversion can be slow, supporting the article's fuel/forex constraint angle. Source: https://www.newsday.co.zw/local-news/article/200054407/pump-revolt-zig-currency-turned-away
- [HIGH] Business Times separately warned that firms paid in ZiG still need USD to restock and import, and may turn to the parallel market if formal access is unreliable. Source: https://businesstimes.co.zw/zig-only-policy-could-fuel-black-market-surge/
- [HIGH] NewsDay/ZimLive reported RBZ assurances that suppliers paid in ZiG can access foreign currency through WBWS and that Zimbabwe had roughly US$16bn in foreign currency receipts in 2025, giving the official counterpoint. Sources: https://www.newsday.co.zw/local-news/article/200052779/suppliers-contractors-to-have-access-to-forex-rbz and https://www.zimlive.com/suppliers-can-access-foreign-currency-despite-zig-payments-rbz/
- [HIGH] ZimStat April inflation was reported by NewsDay and Business Times: ZiG month-on-month inflation rose to 1.1% from 0.5%, while annual ZiG inflation rose to 4.8% from 4.4%. Sources: https://www.newsday.co.zw/local-news/article/200054601/zim-inflation-rises-as-transport-food-costs-squeeze-households and https://businesstimes.co.zw/inflationary-pressures-edge-higher/

## KEY QUOTES

- CZI, via Zimbabwe Independent: "The widening premium can be attributed to heightened demand for foreign currency, as businesses increasingly turn to the parallel market to meet immediate payment obligations."
- NewsDay fuel station source: "We buy our fuel in US dollars."
- Business Times: "Markets do not respond to statements, they respond to consistency, predictability, and access."

## KEY NUMBERS

- Official ZiG movement March to April: +0.9% official appreciation, per CZI as reported by Zimbabwe Independent.
- Parallel ZiG movement March to April: roughly -4.2%, per CZI as reported by Zimbabwe Independent.
- Premium: below 20% in March to just under 25% in April, per CZI as reported by Zimbabwe Independent.
- Inflation: 1.1% MoM in April, 4.8% YoY in April, per ZimStat reporting by NewsDay and Business Times.
- RBZ official position: foreign currency receipts around US$16bn in 2025, per NewsDay/ZimLive reporting of RBZ statement.

## CONTEXT NEEDED FOR READER

The story is not simply that businesses prefer dollars. It is that formal currency stability can coexist with operational USD shortages if companies cannot access dollars quickly enough for fuel, imports and urgent supplier payments. The official market can look stable while the parallel premium quietly becomes the real stress gauge.
