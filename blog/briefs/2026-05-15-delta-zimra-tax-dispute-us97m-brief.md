# Research Brief: Delta-ZIMRA Tax Dispute Hits US$97M

STORY: Delta Corporation, ZIMRA tax dispute hits US$97 million
CLUSTER_ID: delta-zimra-tax-dispute-us97m-may-2026

## KEY FACTS:

- [HIGH] Delta Corporation + Afdis face US$97M cumulative ZIMRA tax assessments, up from US$73M last year (Source: New Zimbabwe + ZiFM, May 15)
- [HIGH] US$24M increase mainly from 2021 year of assessment (Source: ZiFM)
- [HIGH] Delta paid US$18.7M under "pay now, argue later" as of March 31, 2026 (Source: ZiFM + New Zimbabwe)
- [HIGH] Dispute centres on ZIMRA's "turnover-ratio method" applied retroactively (Source: ZiFM)
- [HIGH] Delta argues method was not legally established at the time (Source: ZiFM)
- [HIGH] ZIMRA re-indexing USD liabilities without recognising local currency payments already made (Source: ZiFM)
- [HIGH] CEO Valela: "assessments revalue the liability side without similarly recognising the value already paid" (Source: ZiFM)
- [HIGH] Board Chairman Todd Moyo warned forced cash outflows could strain working capital (Source: New Zimbabwe)
- [HIGH] SI 60 of 2024 could materially change outcome (Source: ZiFM)
- [HIGH] Finance Minister Ncube and PS Guvamatanga called for dialogue (Source: New Zimbabwe)
- [HIGH] Delta paid US$306M in taxes during the year, up 37% (Source: ZiFM)
- [HIGH] Revenue exceeded US$1bn, profit after tax US$151.85M (up from US$112.04M) (Source: ZiFM)
- [HIGH] Foreign-currency turnover increased from 80% to over 94% (Source: ZiFM)
- [MEDIUM] Systemic issue: multiple listed companies face similar retrospective assessments (Source: ZiFM + Zimbabwe Independent)
- [HIGH] High Court and Supreme Court ruled against Delta; Constitutional Court declined to hear appeal (Source: Zimbabwe Independent)

## KEY QUOTES:

- "In 2021 and prior years, we paid our taxes in the legal tender of the day, at the rates required by law. Our view is that the principal obligations were settled, and these assessments revalue the liability side without similarly recognising the value already paid." — Matts Valela, CEO (ZiFM)

- "We believe the best solution does not lie in the courts. The best solution lies in the ministry of Finance giving them proper policy direction because in those years there were conflicting policy directives that were differing to legal provisions, and we followed them to the letter." — Matts Valela, CEO (Zimbabwe Independent)
