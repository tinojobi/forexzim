STORY: Mnangagwa confirms 4.48 tons of gold reserves in latest RBZ visit
CLUSTER_ID: mnangagwa-gold-reserves-rbz-may-2026

KEY FACTS (with confidence tags):
- [HIGH] President Mnangagwa visited RBZ vaults on Monday May 11 and confirmed gold reserves now stand at 4.48 metric tons (Source: New Zimbabwe May 12 + Voice of Nigeria May 12 + ZANU-PF May 12)
- [HIGH] Reserves increased from 4.2 tons held in February 2026 (Source: New Zimbabwe May 12)
- [HIGH] Zimbabwe targets 5 metric tons of gold reserves by end of 2026 (Source: New Zimbabwe May 12 + Voice of Nigeria May 12 + Herald background)
- [HIGH] Zimbabwe now ranks 11th in Africa and 3rd in SADC for official gold reserves (Source: New Zimbabwe May 12 + Voice of Nigeria May 12)
- [HIGH] Annual ZiG inflation stands at 4.8% as of April 2026 (Source: New Zimbabwe May 12 + ZimStat via NewsDay April 27)
- [HIGH] Gold reserves started at 1.5 tonnes when ZiG launched in April 2024 (Source: Mining Zimbabwe March 10 + Herald June 2025 + Midweek Watch March 3)
- [MEDIUM] Gold holdings grew from 1.5t to 4.03t by December 2025, a 250% increase (Source: RBZ Governor Mushayavanhu via Midweek Watch March 3 + Mining Zimbabwe March 10)
- [MEDIUM] Miners pay 50% of royalties in physical gold, 10% in forex, 40% in local currency (Source: Mining Zimbabwe March 10)
- [MEDIUM] 22 billion ZiG currently in circulation (Source: Voice of Nigeria May 12)
- [MEDIUM] Total foreign currency reserves grew from US$276m (April 2024) to US$1.2bn (December 2025) (Source: Mining Zimbabwe March 10)
- [LOW] 326 standard gold bars, each 10kg, 99.5% purity (Source: Herald, from June 2025 inspection, may be outdated)

KEY QUOTES:
- "Following my directive two years ago to accumulate mineral royalties in physical form, our gold reserves have increased considerably." - Mnangagwa
- "These reserves are tangible assets that underpin our monetary sovereignty, rather than mere numbers." - Mnangagwa
- "Gold holdings significantly improved from 1.5 tonnes in April 2024 to 4.03 tonnes by December 2025, representing an increase of over 250%." - RBZ Governor Mushayavanhu
