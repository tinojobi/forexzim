# Research Brief: Zimbabwe Fuel Pricing — Africa's Most Expensive

STORY: Zim imposes Africa's heaviest oil price hikes — shocking rip-off exposed in AU, AfDB regional report
CLUSTER_ID: zimbabwe-fuel-pricing-africa-heaviest-may-2026

## KEY FACTS:

- [HIGH] Zimbabwe has Africa's second most expensive fuel after Malawi (Source: GlobalPetrolPrices via AllAfrica, Business Insider Africa, April 2026)
- [HIGH] Current prices: Petrol US$2.17/litre, Diesel US$2.05/litre (Source: ZERA via NewsDay)
- [HIGH] Highest in SADC region — South Africa US$1.27, Zambia US$1.40, Botswana US$1.15 (Source: Zawya, NewsDay)
- [HIGH] Taxes and levies account for ~US$0.63/litre — nearly 1/3 of pump price (Source: Zawya)
- [HIGH] Excise duty: US$0.30/litre, Strategic Reserve Levy: US$0.127, Carbon Tax: US$0.03, Debt Redemption Levy: US$0.057, ZERA fee: US$0.021 (Source: Zawya)
- [HIGH] Mandatory ethanol blending (E20/E5) — domestic ethanol US$1.10/litre vs global average US$0.60-0.70 (Source: Zawya)
- [HIGH] Green Fuel (Billy Rautenbach) has protected monopoly on ethanol supply (Source: Zawya)
- [HIGH] Government pledged "deliberate actions" in March but no relief materialised (Source: Origins)
- [HIGH] Neighbouring countries implemented cuts: Zambia suspended excise duty, Namibia cut levies 50%, South Africa cut fuel levy by R3/litre (Source: Origins)
- [HIGH] Fuel prices up 40% due to Middle East conflict, Brent crude US$106-115/barrel (Source: NewsDay)
- [HIGH] Fuel dealers only take US$0.06-0.10 profit per litre (Source: NewsDay)
- [HIGH] 80%+ of population depends on informal sector where fuel is essential (Source: Zawya)
