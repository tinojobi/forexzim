# Research Brief: Caledonia Motapa drill results strengthen Bilboes expansion case

## STORY
Caledonia Mining says new high-grade drill results from Motapa, an exploration property next to the Bilboes gold project in Zimbabwe, could extend Bilboes mine life and improve the economics of a future combined mining complex.

## WHY IT MATTERS FOR ZIMRATE
This is a Zimbabwe mining and export story with a direct macro angle. If Motapa adds meaningful nearby ounces to Bilboes, it strengthens the case for a larger future gold-production complex, which matters for export earnings, investor sentiment, and the broader narrative around Zimbabwe's gold sector.

## SOURCE QUALITY NOTE
The scan headline was slightly misleading. The stronger verified framing is not "Bubi project delivers results" in isolation, but rather: **Motapa exploration results could add value to the adjacent Bilboes gold project**. The core facts are corroborated by an official Caledonia source plus two independent reports, with Reuters used for Bilboes financing and production context.

## KEY FACTS
- [HIGH] Motapa sits directly adjacent to the Bilboes gold project and Caledonia says the latest drilling supports the potential for a combined mining complex. Source: Caledonia, Mining Weekly, Yahoo/Proactive.
- [HIGH] Reported drill highlights include 19m at 8.08 g/t gold, 6.38m at 13.95 g/t, 12m at 7.12 g/t, and 14m at 4.31 g/t at Motapa North. Source: Mining Weekly, Yahoo/Proactive, Herald.
- [HIGH] Motapa Central results cited include 7m at 2.39 g/t, 3m at 4.79 g/t, and 2m at 5.25 g/t. Source: Mining Weekly, Yahoo/Proactive, Herald.
- [HIGH] Caledonia expects a maiden mineral resource estimate for parts of Motapa in Q3 2026. Source: Mining Weekly, Yahoo/Proactive.
- [HIGH] Bilboes is targeted for first gold in Q4 2028. Source: Mining Weekly, Herald, Reuters Feb 11.
- [HIGH] Reuters reported in January that Caledonia planned to spend $132 million in 2026 on Bilboes, part of a $162.5 million group capex programme, with Bilboes projected to become Zimbabwe's biggest gold mine. Source: Reuters Jan 14, CNBC Africa/Reuters carry.
- [HIGH] Reuters reported Bilboes is expected to reach steady-state output of 200,000 ounces a year from 2029, with total capital cost estimated at $584 million. Source: Reuters Jan 14 and Feb 11.
- [MEDIUM] The Herald says Motapa drilling confirmed continuity of mineralisation across about 6km of the Bubi Greenstone Belt. This aligns with the independent summaries. Source: Herald, Mining Weekly, Yahoo/Proactive.
- [MEDIUM] Motapa's mining lease covers about 2,200 hectares. Source: Caledonia, Herald.
- [MEDIUM] Historical production from the broader Motapa area may have been as much as 300,000 ounces, according to Caledonia's site background. Source: Caledonia.

## READER CONTEXT NEEDED
- Motapa is not yet a producing mine, it is an exploration property next to Bilboes.
- The immediate commercial project remains Bilboes, not Motapa as a standalone development.
- The real economic significance is that Motapa could improve Bilboes economics through shared infrastructure, more ore, longer mine life, and potentially stronger production later on.
- This is not a production jump today. It is a project-development and future export-capacity story.

## RISKS / THINGS TO AVOID
- Do not write as if Motapa has a defined resource already. Maiden resource is only expected in Q3 2026.
- Do not say production has increased yet. This is exploration news, not output news.
- Do not overstate ZiG/gold-backing implications. Keep that as light context, not the core claim.
- Avoid calling Motapa itself "Zimbabwe's biggest gold mine". That language applies to Bilboes once developed.
