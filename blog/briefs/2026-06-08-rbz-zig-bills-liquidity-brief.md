STORY: RBZ mops up liquidity with new ZiG Bills
CLUSTER_ID: zig-liquidity-sterilisation-2026-06-08

KEY FACTS (with confidence tags):
- [HIGH] RBZ has opened a second offer of ZiG Denominated Term Deposit Facility Bills with 30, 60 and 90-day tenors as part of its open market operations toolkit. (Source: RBZ ZiGDTDF 8 June 2026 PDF + RBZ 2026 Monetary Policy Statement + NewsDay)
- [HIGH] The new bills pay 8% per annum for 30 days, 9% for 60 days, and 11% for 90 days. (Source: RBZ ZiGDTDF 8 June 2026 PDF + NewsDay)
- [HIGH] The instrument is open to banks, building societies, deposit-taking MFIs, POSB, corporates and individuals, which means RBZ is widening participation beyond the banking sector. (Source: RBZ ZiGDTDF 8 June 2026 PDF + NewsDay)
- [HIGH] RBZ says the bills are meant to support reserve money management, exchange rate stability, market-based liquidity management and confidence in ZiG-denominated financial assets under the 2026 Monetary Policy Statement framework. (Source: RBZ ZiGDTDF 8 June 2026 PDF + RBZ 2026 Monetary Policy Statement)
- [HIGH] The offer opened on Monday 8 June 2026 at 08:00, closes on Wednesday 10 June 2026 at 14:00, with settlement and issuance on Thursday 11 June 2026. (Source: RBZ ZiGDTDF 8 June 2026 PDF)
- [MEDIUM] NewsDay frames the move as a response to growing liquidity, exchange-rate pressure and stronger foreign-currency demand linked to import payments, but that explanatory framing should be attributed rather than stated as settled fact. (Source: NewsDay)
- [LOW] NewsDay says the move comes after several months of negative trade balances. This specific claim conflicts with RBZ's own Q1 2026 snapshot, which reports trade surpluses in January and February plus a projected current-account surplus for Q1, so treat it as unverified context and do not use it as a standalone factual claim. (Source: NewsDay vs RBZ Snapshot Q1 2026)

KEY QUOTES (attributed):
- "The Reserve Bank of Zimbabwe (RBZ) has introduced a new round of 30, 60 and 90-day ZiG Denominated Term Deposit Facility Bills (ZiGDTDF), offering interest rates of 8%, 9% and 11% per annum, respectively." - RBZ statement as quoted by NewsDay (NewsDay, 8 June 2026)
- "These Instruments offer positive real interest rate investment opportunities to investors, while at the same time forming part of the RBZ's Open Market Operations toolkit aimed at managing market liquidity conditions and supporting the stability of the Zimbabwe Gold (ZiG)." - Reserve Bank of Zimbabwe (RBZ ZiGDTDF 8 June 2026 PDF)
- "Going forward, the Reserve Bank will continue to align money supply growth with real economic activity, while ensuring that liquidity conditions remain consistent with the inflation objective of preserving ZiG stability and ensuring sustainable economic growth." - Reserve Bank of Zimbabwe (RBZ 2026 Monetary Policy Statement)

CONTEXT NEEDED FOR READER:
- This is not an isolated product launch, it is part of RBZ's broader post-launch ZiG defence strategy: keep liquidity tight, keep exchange-rate expectations calm, and give savers a reason to hold ZiG assets.
- The bigger reader question is whether sterilising liquidity through short-dated bills can support currency stability without starving firms of working capital.
- The move matters because RBZ is leaning more on market-based instruments instead of blunt administrative controls alone.
- The story links directly to everyday Zimbabwe concerns: exchange-rate pressure, import funding, savings incentives, and whether ZiG stability can last.
- Be careful not to overstate the trade-balance justification unless independently re-verified from ZimStat or another official trade release.
