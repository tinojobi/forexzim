# HUMANIZER_RULES.md
## ZimRate Article Humanizer Checklist

These rules apply to every draft before submission. The agent must self-check against this list and rewrite any violations.

---

## 1. BANNED PHRASES (auto-reject if present)

Replace or remove any of these:

- "delve into" / "delving into"
- "navigate the landscape" / "navigating the complex"
- "in today's fast-paced world"
- "it's important to note"
- "it's worth noting"
- "moreover"
- "furthermore"
- "in conclusion"
- "in summary"
- "the world of"
- "the realm of"
- "unlock the potential"
- "robust" (overused)
- "leverage" (as a verb)
- "seamless" / "seamlessly"
- "cutting-edge"
- "game-changer" / "game-changing"
- "in the ever-evolving"
- "stands as a testament"
- "plays a crucial role"
- "at the heart of"
- "a deep dive"
- "tapestry of"
- "myriad of"

---

## 2. BANNED PUNCTUATION

- No em dashes ( -- )
- No en dashes ( - used as a sentence break )
- Use commas, periods, or parentheses instead.

Examples:
- WRONG: "The ZiG fell sharply -- a result of liquidity pressure."
- RIGHT: "The ZiG fell sharply, a result of liquidity pressure."
- WRONG: "Inflation rose 5% -- the highest in months."
- RIGHT: "Inflation rose 5%. That is the highest in months."

---

## 3. SENTENCE RHYTHM

Vary sentence length within every paragraph.

- Mix short (5-10 words), medium (11-20 words), and longer sentences.
- Never write 3 sentences in a row of similar length.
- Aim for at least one short, punchy sentence per paragraph.

Example:
"The RBZ tightened liquidity again this week. Banks now face stricter reserve requirements, which the central bank says will help stabilize the ZiG. Traders are skeptical. Parallel market rates moved up within 48 hours of the announcement."

---

## 4. CONCRETE OVER ABSTRACT

Replace vague claims with specific numbers, names, dates, or places.

- WRONG: "The ZiG experienced significant depreciation."
- RIGHT: "The ZiG fell from 26.10 to 26.50 against the USD between Monday and Friday."

- WRONG: "Many businesses are affected by forex shortages."
- RIGHT: "Bakers, fuel retailers, and small importers report week-long delays sourcing USD."

---

## 5. ACTIVE VOICE DEFAULT

Use active voice unless the actor is genuinely unknown or irrelevant.

- WRONG: "New regulations were introduced by the government."
- RIGHT: "The government introduced new regulations."

- WRONG: "It was announced that ZIMRA will revise tax brackets."
- RIGHT: "ZIMRA announced revised tax brackets."

---

## 6. ONE HUMAN VOICE MARKER PER ARTICLE

Include one of these naturally, not forced:

- A brief rhetorical question (used sparingly): "So what does this mean for someone earning in ZiG?"
- A local reference: "Anyone who shopped at OK Mart this week noticed the price tags moved."
- A grounded observation: "The queue at the Roadport bureau de change tells its own story."
- A short, dry remark: "That is easier said than done."

Only one. Do not pepper the article with these.

---

## 7. SENTENCE OPENERS

Do not start more than 2 sentences in a row with the same word or pattern.

Common AI overuse:
- "The..." (used in every sentence)
- "Zimbabwe..." (repeated as subject)
- "This..." (vague reference)

Vary openers: nouns, verbs, prepositional phrases, time markers (Last week, By Friday, On Monday).

---

## 8. AVOID PROMOTIONAL TONE

This is journalism, not marketing.

- WRONG: "ZimRate is the best tool to track exchange rates."
- RIGHT: "Readers can check current rates on the ZimRate converter."

- WRONG: "Stay tuned for more amazing insights."
- RIGHT: (delete the sentence entirely)

---

## 9. ATTRIBUTION RULES

Every statistic or factual claim needs an inline source.

Format: `(<a href="URL">Source Name</a>)` — always hyperlink to the original article. No dates in citations.

- WRONG: "Inflation reached 12% last month."
- RIGHT: "Inflation reached 12% last month, according to (<a href="https://zimstat.gov.zw/...">ZimStat data published by NewsDay</a>)."

- WRONG: "Gold output reached 46.7 tonnes (Herald, March 2026)."
- RIGHT: "Gold output reached 46.7 tonnes (<a href="https://herald.co.zw/...">Herald</a>)."

If a claim cannot be attributed to a named source, remove it or mark it as "industry estimates".

When multiple citations reference the same source URL, each citation should still be individually hyperlinked.

---

## 10. PARAGRAPH STRUCTURE

- 2 to 4 sentences per paragraph maximum.
- One idea per paragraph.
- No walls of text.

---

## 11. FINAL SELF-CHECK (run before submission)

The agent must answer YES to all of these:

1. Zero banned phrases present?
2. Zero em or en dashes used as breaks?
3. Sentence lengths vary within each paragraph?
4. Every statistic has an inline source?
5. At least one concrete number, name, or place per 100 words?
6. Active voice used in at least 80% of sentences?
7. Exactly one human voice marker present?
8. No paragraph exceeds 4 sentences?
9. Disclaimer footer included?
10. 2 to 3 internal zimrate.com links included?

If any answer is NO, rewrite before submitting.
